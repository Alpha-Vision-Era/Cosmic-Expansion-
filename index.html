The Fluidic Visualization of Galactic Expansion

This work introduces a symbolic but scientifically resonant model of galactic motion. Drawing from the analogy of a punctured water bag, it reimagines Sagittarius A*, the Milky Way’s central black hole, as a gravitational rupture from which structured cosmic expansion flows. The model bridges physical cosmology with metaphysical interpretation, illustrating the universe’s motion as a divine act of ordered release.

Limitations and Scope

While the punctured bag analogy offers an intuitive and philosophically rich framework for interpreting galactic motion and cosmic structure, it is important to acknowledge the conceptual and methodological boundaries of this model. This analogy is intended as a symbolic and cognitive aid, not as a replacement for established scientific theories such as General Relativity, black hole physics, or standard cosmological models. It does not present mathematical modeling, predictive equations, or empirically testable hypotheses that would be required for acceptance within the framework of physical science.

The model's strength lies in its ability to bridge complex cosmic phenomena with accessible physical intuition and metaphysical reflection. However, it simplifies the multi-faceted nature of cosmic evolution, omitting factors such as dark matter, relativistic effects, and quantum gravity phenomena that are essential in modern cosmological understanding.

This work should therefore be read as a conceptual metaphor, aligning physical observations with deeper philosophical and spiritual interpretations of cosmic origin and motion. Future extensions of this analogy could explore quantitative modeling or integrative frameworks that more closely align with empirical cosmological research.
